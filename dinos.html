The Dinosaurs

Here you can learn about some of the most famous dinosaurs featured in the Jurassic Park and Jurassic World films.

Tyrannosaurus Rex

The Tyrannosaurus Rex, or T-Rex, is one of the most powerful predators to ever walk the earth. Famous for its massive size, huge jaws, and tiny arms, the T-Rex is often referred to as the king of the dinosaurs.

Velociraptor

Though much smaller than the T-Rex, the Velociraptor is a highly intelligent and cunning hunter. They hunt in packs and are known for their incredible speed and deadly sickle-shaped claw on each foot.

Triceratops

A peaceful herbivore, the Triceratops is easily recognizable by its three horns and large bony frill. These features are not just for looksâ€”they're used for defense against predators like the T-Rex!

Brachiosaurus

This massive herbivore is known for its extremely long neck and front legs, which were longer than its back legs. They were among the tallest dinosaurs and often ate from the tops of trees.

Dilophosaurus

A small, carnivorous dinosaur with two bony crests on its head. In the movies, it is famously known for its neck frill and ability to spit venom, though this is not scientifically accurate.

Spinosaurus

Easily recognized by the large sail on its back, the Spinosaurus was an enormous predator, even larger than the T-Rex. It was a semi-aquatic dinosaur that primarily hunted fish.

Pteranodon

A large flying reptile, not technically a dinosaur, with a bony crest on its head. Pteranodons soared through the skies and used their long beaks to catch fish.

Compsognathus

These tiny, chicken-sized dinosaurs hunted in large groups, or packs. Their small size and speed made them a threat in large numbers, often overwhelming their prey.

Parasaurolophus

This herbivore is known for its distinctive, long, curved crest on its head. Scientists believe this crest may have been used for communication, allowing them to make loud, low-frequency sounds.

Mosasaurus

A colossal marine reptile, the Mosasaurus was the top predator of the sea. Its immense size and powerful jaws made it a terrifying hunter, capable of taking down even the largest prey.

Indominus Rex

A powerful, genetically engineered hybrid dinosaur created by InGen. It was larger and more intelligent than the T-Rex, with traits from various other dinosaurs, making it a highly dangerous and unpredictable predator.

Indoraptor

Another dangerous hybrid, the Indoraptor was a smaller but more agile and intelligent version of the Indominus Rex. It was designed to be a weapon and was incredibly vicious and cunning.

Allosaurus

A large carnivorous dinosaur known for its massive skull and saw-like teeth. It was a formidable predator, often hunting in packs to take down large prey.

Ankylosaurus

This herbivore was like a living tank, covered in thick bony armor and a club on the end of its tail. This heavy defense made it nearly invulnerable to most predators.

Carnotaurus

A unique-looking predator with two distinctive horns above its eyes and extremely short arms. It was known for its incredible speed, making it a dangerous ambush hunter.

Gallimimus

A long-necked, ostrich-like dinosaur that was built for speed. It was a quick and agile herbivore that traveled in large herds.

Stegosaurus

This herbivore is easily identifiable by the large, bony plates along its back and the spiked tail it used for defense against predators.

Pachycephalosaurus

Known as the "dome-headed" dinosaur, this herbivore had a thick, bony skull that was likely used for head-butting with other members of its species.

Baryonyx

A fish-eating dinosaur with a long, crocodile-like snout and a massive claw on each hand. It was a formidable predator that lived near water sources.

Dimorphodon

A flying reptile with a large head and two different types of teeth. Like the Pteranodon, it was not technically a dinosaur but was a prominent part of the park's ecosystem.
