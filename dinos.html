The Prehistoric Creatures of Jurassic Park

Here is a complete list of the amazing prehistoric animals that have appeared throughout the Jurassic franchise.

Allosaurus

A large theropod dinosaur, known for its powerful jaws and distinctive bony crests above its eyes.

Ankylosaurus

Often called a "living tank," this dinosaur is covered in bony armor and has a massive club at the end of its tail.

Anurognathus (pterosaur)

A small, insect-eating flying reptile with a very short tail.

Apatosaurus

A gigantic, long-necked sauropod, often confused with the Brachiosaurus. They are massive, peaceful plant-eaters.

Aquilops

A small, horned dinosaur, notable for being one of the earliest known ceratopsians.

Atrociraptor

A fierce and aggressive raptor that is known for its incredible speed and relentless hunting.

Baryonyx

A piscivorous (fish-eating) dinosaur with a crocodile-like snout and a large claw on its thumb.

Becklespinax

A large theropod dinosaur, noted for its tall spines on the vertebrae.

Brachiosaurus

A massive, long-necked herbivore. The Brachiosaurus is one of the first dinosaurs seen in the park and is known for its peaceful nature.

Carnotaurus

A fast, powerful predator with two distinctive horns above its eyes. It's a quick and aggressive hunter.

Ceratosaurus

A carnivorous theropod known for the horn on its snout. It's an aggressive but smaller predator.

Compsognathus

Small, fast, and social carnivores that hunt in large packs. They are known for being cunning and dangerous in groups.

Corythosaurus

A duck-billed dinosaur with a tall, helmet-like crest on its head, which it may have used for making loud sounds.

Dilophosaurus

Recognizable by the frill around its neck and its ability to spit venom. It's a smaller predator but still very dangerous.

Dimetrodon (synapsid)

A prehistoric reptile-like creature with a large sail on its back, famous for being a powerful carnivore.

Dimorphodon (pterosaur)

A small pterosaur with a large head, a short neck, and two different types of teeth. It's often found flying in flocks.

Distortus rex

A deadly hybrid dinosaur, the result of genetic engineering, known for its extreme aggression.

Dreadnoughtus

An immense, long-necked sauropod, one of the largest land animals ever discovered.

Dunkleosteus (fish)

An ancient armored fish with powerful jaws and a huge size, living in prehistoric oceans.

Gallimimus

A fast-running herbivore that looks a bit like an ostrich. They are known for their speed and herd behavior.

Giant Lamprey (fish)

A large, parasitic prehistoric fish with a circular mouth full of teeth.

Giant Locust (insect)

Genetically modified, oversized locusts that cause global crop destruction.

Giganotosaurus

One of the largest land carnivores, this dinosaur rivals the T. rex in size and is a top predator.

Iguanodon

A large, herbivorous dinosaur known for its distinctive thumb spikes, which it used for defense.

Indominus rex

A genetically engineered hybrid dinosaur with a mix of different dinosaur traits. It's bigger, smarter, and more ferocious than a T. rex.

Indoraptor

A deadly hybrid dinosaur, a smaller but smarter version of the Indominus Rex. It's a master of stealth and can hunt its prey with a single command.

Kentrosaurus

A dinosaur with plates on its back and long, menacing spikes on its tail and shoulders, used for defense.

Lystrosaurus (therapsid)

A mammal-like reptile that lived before the dinosaurs, known for its small size and two tusks.

Majungasaurus

A theropod dinosaur from Madagascar, known for its single horn on its head and aggressive nature.

Mamenchisaurus

A huge, long-necked sauropod, famous for having one of the longest necks of any dinosaur.

Microceratus

A small ceratopsian dinosaur, known for its small size and frill. It's one of the tiniest horned dinosaurs.

Monolophosaurus

A medium-sized theropod known for a single, distinctive crest on its snout.

Moros

A small tyrannosauroid dinosaur, thought to be a distant ancestor of the T. rex.

Mosasaurus (mosasaur)

An enormous marine reptile that lives in the Jurassic World lagoon. It's a huge carnivore that eats sharks and other creatures.

Mutadon

A genetically engineered dinosaur with a mix of different dinosaur traits. It's a powerful and dangerous hybrid.

Nasutoceratops

A large, herbivorous dinosaur with a pair of long, curved horns over its face, similar to a bull.

Nothosaurus (nothosaur)

A semi-aquatic reptile with a long body and a powerful tail, living in prehistoric seas.

Ouranosaurus

A herbivorous dinosaur with a large sail on its back, a duck-like beak, and thumb spikes.

Oviraptor

A small, feathered dinosaur with a parrot-like beak, often shown as a clever and mischievous creature.

Pachycephalosaurus

Known for its thick, domed skull, which it may have used for head-butting rivals or as a defense.

Pachyrhinosaurus

A large ceratopsian dinosaur with a bony boss over its nose instead of a horn, as well as a large neck frill.

Parasaurolophus

A large, herbivorous dinosaur with a distinctive, curved crest on its head that it may have used for communication.

Pteranodon (pterosaur)

A flying reptile with a long crest on its head. Pteranodons live in giant aviaries and can be very aggressive.

Pyroraptor

A small, feathered, and highly agile raptor known for its intelligence and speed.

Quetzalcoatlus (pterosaur)

One of the largest flying animals ever, this gigantic pterosaur soars through the skies and is a powerful predator.

Scorpios rex

A ferocious and unstable hybrid dinosaur, known for its poisonous quills and unpredictable behavior.

Sinoceratops

A large, horned dinosaur with a distinctive curved frill. It's a peaceful herbivore and is often seen in herds.

Smilodon (mammal)

A saber-toothed cat, a powerful prehistoric mammal with massive fangs.

Spinoceratops

A hybrid dinosaur combining the traits of Spinosaurus and Sinoceratops, creating a deadly and unique predator.

Spinosaurus

Known for the huge sail on its back, this dinosaur is even larger than the T. rex and is a skilled hunter in both water and on land.

Stegosaurus

A large, plant-eating dinosaur with bony plates along its back and a spiked tail for defense.

Stygimoloch

A smaller dinosaur with horns and a spiky skull, sometimes debated by scientists as being a younger form of Pachycephalosaurus.

Suchomimus

A large, fish-eating dinosaur with a long, narrow snout and a low sail on its back.

Tarbosaurus

A large, carnivorous dinosaur from Asia, closely related to the Tyrannosaurus rex.

Therizinosaurus

A large, herbivorous dinosaur with incredibly long claws on its hands.

Titanosaurus

A group of large, long-necked sauropods known for their wide distribution around the world.

Triceratops

A large, herbivorous dinosaur with three horns on its face. It's gentle unless provoked, and a favorite of many park visitors.

Tyrannosaurus rex

One of the largest and most powerful carnivores, the T. rex is the star of the show. Famous for its massive size and iconic roar.

Unidentified Dinosaur Fetuses

The unhatched dinosaur eggs and embryos that are often seen in the park's labs and hatcheries.

Unnamed Large Amphibian (amphibian)

A large, unknown prehistoric amphibian seen in the animated series.

Unnamed Eel/Snake (fish/snake)

A mysterious, eel-like or snake-like aquatic creature featured in the series.

Unnamed Ornithopod Carcass

The remains of an unknown herbivorous dinosaur, a clue to what roams the island.

Velociraptor

Highly intelligent and social pack hunters, the raptors are known for their clever tactics and sharp claws.
